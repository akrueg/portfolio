Resume & Web Portfolio of Andy Krueger{#spanMyName}

I developed the samples below using CodePen (and Cloud9 for Sample 3) as educational projects, to help me learn about / experiment with various aspects of front-end (and some back-end) web development. They were developed in Chromium{green!} & Firefox{green!} on a Linux (Ubuntu) desktop and Chrome for Android{green!} on a Samsung phone; I haven't yet been able to work on them in Safari{red} or IE / Edge{red}.

Sample 1: Analysis of touch events

This page is a tool for inspecting the browser's touch-event behavior. If you touch the main green <div>, it reports details on the touch events and synthetic mouse events triggered, below which is some analysis. The page uses a combination of MarkDown and disclosure triangles to communicate a lot of information in a clean, readable format that looks good on a mobile or desktop screen.

Sample 2: Inline formatting, part 1

Part 2.1 2.2 2.3 3.1 3.2 3.3 4

This series examines how the browser implements line & inline boxes, line-height, em size, vertical-align, and other details of text rendering. It relies on several technical strategies including

  • use of FontForge to design fonts with experimental metrics and a custom glyph

  • base64-encoded fonts and graphics

  • use of svg to highlight parts of characters

  • a series of interactive demos.

Sample 3: Flexbox popup

I'm including this page mainly for its friendlier feel, compared to the more academic style of the other samples. This was one of my early projects, and I was pleasantly surprised by how easy it was to implement the clickable, scalable, light-up svg-map; then I fell off a cliff of css madness trying to accomplish vertical centering (of the popup), until I got to know flexboxes.
